About
Maple trees are widely admired for their stunning seasonal colors and distinctive leaf shapes.
Found across North America, Europe, and Asia, maples are a popular choice for urban landscaping and natural woodlands alike.
Their leaves often turn brilliant shades of red, orange, and yellow in the fall, making them a highlight of autumn scenery. In addition to their beauty, some species—like the sugar maple—are prized for producing maple syrup.
Hardy and adaptable, maple trees thrive in a range of climates and soil types.

Fir trees are evergreen conifers known for their tall, symmetrical shape and fragrant needles. Commonly found in mountainous and cooler regions, firs play a key role in forest ecosystems and are a favorite choice for holiday trees during the winter season.
Their needles are soft and flat, and unlike other evergreens, they grow directly from the branches in a spiral pattern. Fir wood is also valued for construction and paper production. With their year-round greenery and classic shape, fir trees add beauty and structure to any landscape.
